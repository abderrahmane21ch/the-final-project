Cristiano Ronaldo Career

Manchester United

As a way of silencing English and some United fans, CR7 vowed to keep scoring goals. In no time, he broke the 20-goal barrier for the first time and won his first Premier League title.
Several collective and individual successes including his first FIFA Ballon d’Or, followed.

Cristiano Ronaldo To Real Madrid

Ahead of the 2009–10 season, CR7 joined Real Madrid for a world-record transfer fee at the time — £80 million (€94 million) with a €1 billion buy-out clause.

At Los Blancos, he came back to his Ballon d’Or-winning form — which he won consecutively.

Real Madrid
And Juventus

Scoring 451 times in 438 matches with a host of trophies and honours turned Ronnie into a self-acclaimed Football GOAT. Like Alexia Putellas, he won more than one Ballon d’Or while playing for a Spain team.
Following a fallout with Los Blancos president Florentino Perez, Cristiano felt he was no longer indispensable. This made him number his days with Real Madrid.
Massimiliano Allegri tried his luck and shockingly won the jackpot by signing the Legend to Juventus. At Juve, Cristiano began breaking national and club goal scoring records.
To unify his honour, he even led Portugal to gather more national trophies and honours to his cabinet.
